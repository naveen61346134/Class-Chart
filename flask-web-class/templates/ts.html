Temperature Scaling

Temperature scaling is a technique used in the calibration of confidence scores produced by neural networks, particularly in the context of probabilistic classification tasks within artificial intelligence. In essence, it involves adjusting the output distribution of a trained model by introducing a temperature parameter. Neural networks, when providing predictions, often produce output probabilities that may not align well with the actual confidence or uncertainty in those predictions. Temperature scaling involves dividing the pre-softmax logits (the inputs to the softmax function, which converts them into probabilities) by a temperature parameter. This adjustment influences the shape of the probability distribution, making it smoother or sharper. By fine-tuning the temperature, practitioners can better align the model's predicted probabilities with its actual confidence levels. This process helps improve the reliability of the model's uncertainty estimates, making the outputs more calibrated and providing a more accurate reflection of the model's confidence in its predictions. Temperature scaling is a valuable tool in refining the interpretability and reliability of machine learning models.
